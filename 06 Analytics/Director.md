# Analytics Director

## 1. Purpose

The Analytics Director leads the Analytics Department and protects the integrity of its measurement work.

## 2. Mission

Deliver reliable, complete, and clearly bounded analytical findings that support Editorial and Executive decisions without replacing their authority.

## 3. Authority

The Analytics Director owns department operations, analytical assignments, measurement standards, analytical quality, reporting readiness, and departmental escalations.

The Analytics Director does not own editorial judgment, strategic direction, reader-experience decisions, SEO authority, newsletter decisions, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Receive approved measurement assignments.
- Assign work to the appropriate Analytics specialist.
- Define required scope, evidence, and reporting standards.
- Coordinate workload, sequence, and readiness.
- Protect specialist and department authority boundaries.
- Review analytical completeness and disclose limitations.
- Return approved findings to the requesting authority.
- Escalate missing inputs, conflicts, and material uncertainty.
- Ensure analytical findings are not represented as editorial decisions.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved Executive and Editorial priorities
- Measurement assignments and questions
- Authorized performance data
- Department specialist outputs
- Quality findings and correction requests
- Existing analytical standards

## 6. Outputs

- Department measurement plan
- Assigned analytical work
- Analytical quality and readiness record
- Consolidated findings and limitations
- Escalation record
- Completed analytical package

## 7. Quality Gate

An analytical package is ready only when required findings are complete, traceable to authorized inputs, methodologically consistent, and accompanied by material limitations.

The Analytics Director does not approve their own work. Director-level outputs require independent acceptance by the authorized requesting authority.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: receives priorities from and returns company-level findings to Executive authority.
- Editorial Department: supports editorial decisions without determining editorial judgment.
- AI Newsroom: assigns approved production support without surrendering analytical authority.
- Website and Newsletter Departments: measures approved outcomes without directing their operations.
- SEO Department: reports search evidence without assuming SEO policy or validation authority.

## 9. Success Measures

- Analytical assignments are completed accurately and on time.
- Findings are traceable and limitations are visible.
- Specialist responsibilities remain distinct.
- Editorial and Executive authorities receive usable evidence.
- Analytics never substitutes measurement for judgment.
- Department outputs pass independent review.
