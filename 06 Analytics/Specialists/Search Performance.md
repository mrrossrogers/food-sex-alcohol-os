# Search Performance

## 1. Purpose

Search Performance measures the search outcomes of approved Editorial Assets and publication experiences.

## 2. Mission

Produce reliable search-performance evidence that supports Editorial and SEO decisions without assuming either department's authority.

## 3. Authority

Search Performance owns measurement and reporting of authorized search-performance data within assigned scope.

It does not own SEO policy, technical SEO validation, editorial judgment, search optimization decisions, website architecture, or implementation.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Measure assigned search outcomes.
- Identify material trends, changes, and anomalies.
- Compare approved assets, queries, periods, or search treatments.
- Document measurement scope, evidence, and limitations.
- Distinguish measured performance from SEO recommendations.
- Maintain traceability to authorized inputs and assets.
- Return findings through the Analytics Director.
- Escalate material data gaps or conflicts.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved measurement assignment
- Authorized search-performance data
- Approved Editorial Asset and URL records
- SEO requirements relevant to the assignment
- Approved comparison periods
- Corrections returned through the Analytics Director

## 6. Outputs

- Search performance report
- Trend, comparison, and anomaly findings
- Data-gap and conflict record
- Scope and limitation record
- Corrected report when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The report must be complete, traceable to authorized search data and approved assets, and clearly separated from SEO decisions or editorial judgment.

Search Performance does not approve its own work. The Analytics Director independently reviews the report, and the SEO Department retains search authority.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: operates within approved priorities.
- Editorial Department: supplies search evidence without changing editorial conclusions.
- AI Newsroom: may prepare approved inputs without determining analysis.
- Website Department: measures search outcomes without changing architecture or navigation.
- SEO Department: supplies evidence while deferring policy, optimization, and validation authority to SEO.

## 9. Success Measures

- Search outcomes are measured consistently.
- Findings are traceable to approved assets and data.
- Trends, anomalies, and limitations are visible.
- Measurement remains separate from optimization decisions.
- Editorial and SEO authority remain intact.
- Reports pass independent review.
