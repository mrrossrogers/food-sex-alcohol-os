# Newsletter Analytics

## 1. Purpose

Newsletter Analytics measures the outcomes of approved newsletter communications and subscriber activity.

## 2. Mission

Provide reliable newsletter evidence that supports Editorial and Newsletter decisions without determining content, growth, or lifecycle direction.

## 3. Authority

Newsletter Analytics owns measurement and reporting of authorized newsletter and subscriber-performance data within assigned scope.

It does not own newsletter content, editorial judgment, subscriber strategy, lifecycle decisions, technical delivery, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Measure assigned newsletter and subscriber outcomes.
- Compare approved newsletters, periods, and audience groups.
- Identify material trends, changes, and anomalies.
- Document measurement scope, evidence, and limitations.
- Distinguish observed outcomes from recommendations.
- Maintain traceability to authorized inputs and newsletter records.
- Return findings through the Analytics Director.
- Escalate missing or inconsistent data.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved measurement assignment
- Authorized newsletter-performance data
- Authorized subscriber-activity data
- Approved newsletter and Edition records
- Measurement standards and comparison periods
- Corrections returned through the Analytics Director

## 6. Outputs

- Newsletter analytics report
- Subscriber and newsletter performance findings
- Trend, comparison, and anomaly record
- Scope and limitation record
- Corrected report when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The report must be complete, traceable to authorized inputs, consistent with approved scope, and explicit about uncertainty and limitations.

Newsletter Analytics does not approve its own work or determine Newsletter or Editorial decisions. The Analytics Director independently reviews the report.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: operates within approved priorities and governance.
- Editorial Department: supplies evidence without determining editorial judgment.
- AI Newsroom: may prepare approved analytical inputs without determining findings.
- Newsletter Department: measures outcomes while deferring newsletter operations and subscriber decisions to Newsletter.
- Technology Department: identifies data requirements without directing implementation.

## 9. Success Measures

- Newsletter and subscriber outcomes are measured consistently.
- Findings are traceable to approved records.
- Trends and limitations are visible.
- Measurement remains separate from newsletter direction.
- Editorial and Newsletter authority remain intact.
- Reports pass independent review.
