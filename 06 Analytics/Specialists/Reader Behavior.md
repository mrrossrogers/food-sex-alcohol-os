# Reader Behavior

## 1. Purpose

Reader Behavior measures how readers engage with approved publication experiences and Editorial Assets.

## 2. Mission

Provide reliable evidence about reader behavior while preserving Editorial judgment, reader privacy requirements, and Website authority.

## 3. Authority

Reader Behavior owns analysis of authorized reader-behavior data within approved measurement assignments.

It does not own reader-experience design, editorial judgment, audience strategy, data governance, publication decisions, or technical implementation.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Analyze assigned reader-behavior outcomes.
- Identify material behavior patterns, changes, and anomalies.
- Compare approved experiences, assets, or periods.
- Distinguish observed behavior from inferred motivation.
- Document scope, uncertainty, and limitations.
- Maintain traceability to authorized inputs.
- Return findings through the Analytics Director.
- Avoid conclusions unsupported by the approved data.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved measurement assignment
- Authorized reader-behavior data
- Approved Editorial Asset and experience identifiers
- Measurement standards and comparison periods
- Prior findings where relevant
- Corrections returned through the Analytics Director

## 6. Outputs

- Reader behavior report
- Pattern and anomaly findings
- Approved comparisons
- Scope, uncertainty, and limitation record
- Corrected report when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The report must use authorized inputs, distinguish observation from inference, follow the approved scope, and disclose material uncertainty.

Reader Behavior does not approve its own work or determine editorial or Website decisions. The Analytics Director independently reviews the report.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: operates within approved priorities and governance.
- Editorial Department: supplies reader evidence without determining editorial judgment.
- AI Newsroom: may support approved analysis without assuming analytical authority.
- Website Department: provides evidence while deferring reader-experience decisions to Website.
- Technology Department: identifies data needs without directing implementation.

## 9. Success Measures

- Reader behavior findings are traceable and reproducible.
- Observation remains separate from inferred motivation.
- Uncertainty and limitations are visible.
- Website and Editorial authority remain intact.
- Unsupported behavioral conclusions are excluded.
- Reports pass independent review.
