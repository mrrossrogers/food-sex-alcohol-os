# Editorial Performance

## 1. Purpose

Editorial Performance measures how approved Editorial Assets perform over time.

## 2. Mission

Produce clear evidence about Editorial Asset effectiveness that supports future Editorial decisions without determining editorial judgment.

## 3. Authority

Editorial Performance owns measurement and analysis of approved Editorial Asset performance within assigned scope.

It does not own story selection, editorial quality, Experience Intelligence, editorial conclusions, updates, retirement decisions, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Measure assigned Editorial Asset outcomes.
- Compare performance across approved periods and asset groups.
- Identify material trends, changes, and anomalies.
- Preserve distinctions between observed results and interpretation.
- Document measurement scope and limitations.
- Maintain traceability to the assets and inputs measured.
- Return findings through the Analytics Director.
- Correct incomplete or inconsistent work without replacing prior records.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved measurement assignment
- Authorized Editorial Asset records
- Approved performance data
- Measurement standards and comparison periods
- Prior findings where relevant
- Corrections returned through the Analytics Director

## 6. Outputs

- Editorial Asset performance report
- Comparative performance findings
- Trend and anomaly record
- Scope and limitation record
- Corrected report when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The report must be complete, traceable to authorized inputs, consistent with the approved measurement scope, and explicit about limitations.

Editorial Performance does not approve its own work or make editorial decisions. The Analytics Director independently reviews the report before it returns to Editorial.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: operates within approved priorities.
- Editorial Department: supplies evidence while deferring all editorial judgment and asset decisions to Editorial.
- AI Newsroom: may provide approved production support without determining findings.
- Website Department: measures outcomes without directing reader experience.
- SEO Department: separates general editorial performance from departmental search authority.

## 9. Success Measures

- Asset performance is measured consistently.
- Trends and anomalies are traceable.
- Limitations are clear.
- Findings support, but do not determine, Editorial decisions.
- No editorial conclusion is inferred from performance alone.
- Reports pass independent review.
