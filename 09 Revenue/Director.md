# Revenue Director

## 1. Purpose

The Revenue Director leads the Revenue Department and protects the absolute separation between commercial work and editorial judgment.

## 2. Mission

Execute authorized revenue opportunities that support the publication without influencing Editorial work, conclusions, recommendations, or publication decisions.

## 3. Authority

The Revenue Director owns department operations, authorized commercial assignments, partner readiness, membership readiness, commercial conformance, and operational escalations.

The Revenue Director does not own editorial judgment, story selection, editorial conclusions, company strategy, publication approval, reader experience, or technical implementation.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Receive Executive-authorized revenue assignments.
- Confirm that each opportunity follows approved Editorial work.
- Assign work to the appropriate Revenue specialist.
- Coordinate commercial dependencies, readiness, and records.
- Protect Editorial independence and department boundaries.
- Reject or escalate any request to alter Editorial work for commercial interests.
- Confirm required approvals and disclosures before activity begins.
- Coordinate approved production support through the AI Newsroom.
- Escalate conflicts, missing authorization, and integrity risks.

## 5. Inputs

- Executive-authorized revenue assignments
- Approved Editorial work where applicable
- Approved partner, membership, or commerce requirements
- Department specialist outputs
- Required approvals and disclosures
- Quality findings and correction requests

## 6. Outputs

- Revenue execution plan
- Assigned department work
- Commercial readiness and conformance record
- Editorial independence check
- Complete authorized revenue package
- Escalation record

## 7. Quality Gate

A revenue package is ready only when Executive authorization, required disclosures, commercial requirements, and Editorial independence are complete and documented.

The Revenue Director does not approve their own work or any editorial content. Director-level outputs require independent acceptance by the authorized reviewing authority.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: receives authorization from and escalates strategic or commercial decisions to Executive authority.
- Editorial Department: follows approved Editorial work and never influences editorial judgment or conclusions.
- AI Newsroom: assigns approved production support without surrendering Revenue authority.
- Analytics Department: receives authorized evidence without transferring Revenue decisions.
- Website and Technology Departments: supplies approved requirements without assuming experience or implementation authority.

## 9. Success Measures

- Revenue work is authorized and traceable.
- Editorial independence remains absolute.
- No Editorial work is modified for commercial interests.
- Required approvals and disclosures are complete.
- Specialists remain within their authority.
- Revenue packages pass independent review.
