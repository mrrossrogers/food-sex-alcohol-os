# Membership

## 1. Purpose

Membership manages authorized membership operations that support the publication.

## 2. Mission

Deliver approved membership communications and benefits without influencing Editorial judgment, conclusions, access decisions reserved to Editorial, or publication priorities.

## 3. Authority

Membership owns execution of approved membership assignments, member communication requirements, benefit records, and membership-status coordination.

It does not own membership strategy, editorial judgment, editorial content, company governance, subscriber data governance, technical implementation, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Execute only Executive-authorized membership assignments.
- Maintain approved benefit, eligibility, communication, and status records.
- Use only approved Editorial and membership materials.
- Preserve Editorial independence and reader trust.
- Identify missing authorization, conflicting requirements, or unsupported promises.
- Reject and escalate requests to modify Editorial work for membership interests.
- Confirm required approvals before member activity begins.
- Submit completed records through the Revenue Director.

## 5. Inputs

- Executive-authorized membership assignment
- Approved membership requirements and benefits
- Approved Editorial and communication materials
- Authorized eligibility and status requirements
- Required approvals and disclosures
- Corrections returned through the Revenue Director

## 6. Outputs

- Membership operations package
- Benefit and eligibility record
- Approved member communication package
- Membership-status record
- Exception and escalation record

## 7. Quality Gate

Membership work must be authorized, complete, accurate, consistent with approved benefits and communications, and free of influence over Editorial work.

Membership does not approve its own work or editorial content. The Revenue Director and applicable reviewing authority independently review the package.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: executes only approved membership priorities and requirements.
- Editorial Department: uses approved Editorial work without influencing editorial decisions or priorities.
- AI Newsroom: may produce approved membership-support assets without determining membership or Editorial policy.
- Newsletter Department: coordinates approved member communications without changing Newsletter authority.
- Technology Department: states approved operational needs without directing implementation.

## 9. Success Measures

- Membership work is authorized and traceable.
- Benefits, eligibility, communications, and status records are complete.
- Editorial independence remains intact.
- Unsupported promises and conflicts are escalated.
- Member communications use approved materials.
- Packages pass independent review.
