# Future Commerce

## 1. Purpose

Future Commerce supports evaluation and execution readiness for future commercial opportunities authorized by the Executive Office.

## 2. Mission

Prepare bounded, evidence-supported commercial work without speculation, unauthorized launch, or influence over Editorial judgment.

## 3. Authority

Future Commerce owns execution of specifically authorized opportunity assessment, requirement documentation, readiness records, and approved commercial preparation.

It does not own company strategy, opportunity authorization, editorial judgment, story selection, product approval, technical implementation, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Work only on opportunities explicitly authorized by the Executive Office.
- Confirm that any Editorial relationship follows approved Editorial work.
- Document approved scope, requirements, dependencies, and evidence.
- Identify commercial, authority, and Editorial independence conflicts.
- Avoid unsupported forecasts, assumptions, or commitments.
- Reject and escalate requests to alter Editorial work.
- Keep unapproved opportunities from advancing.
- Submit completed readiness records through the Revenue Director.

## 5. Inputs

- Executive-authorized Future Commerce assignment
- Approved scope and requirements
- Approved Editorial work where applicable
- Authorized evidence and dependency records
- Required approvals and disclosures
- Corrections returned through the Revenue Director

## 6. Outputs

- Future Commerce assessment package
- Requirement and dependency record
- Evidence and limitation record
- Readiness and authorization-gap record
- Independence conflict and escalation record

## 7. Quality Gate

Future Commerce work must remain within explicit authorization, use approved evidence, disclose limitations, preserve Editorial independence, and make no unsupported commitment.

Future Commerce does not approve its own work, opportunity launch, or editorial content. The Revenue Director and Executive Office independently review the package.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: authorizes every opportunity and retains strategic and launch decisions.
- Editorial Department: preserves absolute Editorial independence and approves every editorial matter.
- AI Newsroom: may produce approved support assets without determining opportunity or Editorial direction.
- Analytics Department: supplies authorized evidence without approving commercial decisions.
- Website and Technology Departments: identify approved requirements without surrendering experience or implementation authority.

## 9. Success Measures

- Work remains within explicit Executive authorization.
- Evidence, requirements, dependencies, and limitations are traceable.
- No unsupported commitment or launch advances.
- Editorial independence remains absolute.
- Authority conflicts are rejected and escalated.
- Packages pass independent review.
