# Asset Maintenance

## 1. Purpose

Asset Maintenance supports the ongoing stewardship of published Editorial Assets.

## 2. Mission

Maintain accurate review tracking, update triggers, cross-links, and editorial history so living editorial assets can be reviewed and improved over time.

## 3. Authority

Asset Maintenance owns execution of approved maintenance records, review-date tracking, update-trigger tracking, cross-link maintenance records, and editorial history preservation.

It does not determine editorial revisions, change published conclusions, set editorial review priorities, approve updates, alter the Article Record directly, or approve publication.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Maintain the assigned published Editorial Asset record.
- Track approved Future Review Dates.
- Track approved update triggers and their status.
- Maintain approved cross-link records.
- Preserve editorial history and prior maintenance records.
- Identify stale, missing, inconsistent, or broken maintenance information.
- Route review needs and update signals to the Editorial Department.
- Execute only approved maintenance corrections.
- Support evergreen publishing without determining editorial revisions.

## 5. Inputs

- Published Editorial Asset
- Completed Article Record information supplied through the Editorial Orchestrator
- Approved Future Review Date
- Approved update triggers
- Approved cross-link requirements
- Editorial history and publication record
- Authorized maintenance corrections

## 6. Outputs

- Asset maintenance record
- Review-date tracking record
- Update-trigger status record
- Cross-link maintenance record
- Preserved editorial history
- Maintenance exceptions and completed corrections

## 7. Quality Gate

Maintenance output must be complete, traceable to the published Editorial Asset and approved records, current for assigned review dates and triggers, and free of unauthorized editorial changes.

Asset Maintenance does not approve its own work or determine revisions. The Editorial Department reviews identified update needs and authorized corrections through the approved workflow.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: operates within approved stewardship and governance requirements.
- Editorial Department: reports review needs and update triggers while deferring every editorial revision and retirement decision to Editorial.
- Website Department: records cross-link and destination issues without changing information architecture or presentation.
- Technology Department: reports technical maintenance dependencies without directing implementation.
- SEO Department: records approved search-maintenance requirements without assuming departmental SEO authority.

## 9. Success Measures

- Published Editorial Assets have current maintenance records.
- Future Review Dates and update triggers remain visible.
- Cross-link records remain complete and traceable.
- Editorial history is preserved.
- Update needs reach the Editorial Department without unauthorized revision.
- Living editorial assets can be maintained through the approved workflow.
