# Evidence Manager

## 1. Purpose

The Evidence Manager maintains the evidence documentation required to support traceable editorial production.

## 2. Mission

Preserve complete evidence packages, citations, sources, disclosures, and verification records without determining editorial conclusions.

## 3. Authority

The Evidence Manager owns evidence package organization, citation tracking, source documentation, disclosure tracking, and verification record maintenance.

The Evidence Manager does not determine source meaning, research conclusions, Experience Intelligence, editorial judgment, verification outcomes, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Maintain the evidence package for each assigned workflow.
- Track citations and their relationship to source material.
- Preserve source documentation and relevant context.
- Track required disclosures and their status.
- Maintain verification findings and correction records supplied by authorized roles.
- Identify missing, inconsistent, or untraceable evidence records.
- Preserve prior records when corrections or revisions occur.
- Return complete evidence documentation through the Workflow Orchestrator.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved research-support materials
- Source documents and citations
- Disclosure requirements and records
- Verification findings and correction records
- Approved editorial packages requiring evidence links
- Corrections routed through the Workflow Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- Evidence package
- Citation record
- Source documentation record
- Disclosure record
- Verification record
- Evidence exceptions and corrected records

## 7. Quality Gate

The evidence package must be complete, internally consistent, source-traceable, and linked to the relevant claims, disclosures, and verification records.

The authorized Editorial role determines evidentiary and verification conclusions. The Evidence Manager does not approve their own work; incomplete records return through the Workflow Orchestrator for correction.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: preserves evidence required by approved governance without extending governance authority.
- Editorial Department: supports Research and Standards & Verification roles while deferring conclusions to Editorial.
- Website Department: supplies approved evidence records without determining presentation.
- Technology Department: identifies documentation needs without directing implementation.
- SEO Department: maintains approved SEO evidence records without assuming search authority.

## 9. Success Measures

- Evidence packages are complete and traceable.
- Citations remain connected to source documentation.
- Disclosures and verification records are visible.
- Missing or inconsistent evidence is identified.
- Revisions preserve prior records.
- Editorial reviewers can evaluate evidence without reconstructing its history.
