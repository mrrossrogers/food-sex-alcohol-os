# Draft Production

## 1. Purpose

Draft Production creates working drafts from approved Editorial Briefs and approved supporting packages.

## 2. Mission

Produce complete working drafts that faithfully execute approved editorial direction without inventing facts, changing conclusions, or bypassing verification.

## 3. Authority

Draft Production owns execution of the assigned working draft, including assembly, structure, and expression within the approved Editorial Brief.

It does not own editorial direction, Experience Intelligence findings, research conclusions, verification, final copy approval, SEO decisions, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Use only the approved Editorial Brief, research, and Experience Intelligence findings.
- Produce the complete assigned working draft.
- Preserve approved facts, meaning, voice, direction, and required distinctions.
- Identify missing information rather than inventing content.
- Keep unsupported claims out of the draft.
- Record dependencies, unresolved questions, and source gaps.
- Submit the draft for required quality checks and Editorial review.
- Revise only in response to authorized corrections routed through the Workflow Orchestrator.
- Never bypass verification.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved Editorial Brief
- Approved research materials
- Approved Experience Intelligence findings
- Required structure and deliverables
- Approved editorial direction
- Evidence package
- Corrections routed through the Workflow Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- Working draft
- Draft production package
- Recorded dependencies and unresolved questions
- Source-gap record
- Corrected working draft when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The working draft must be complete, structurally consistent with the approved brief, faithful to approved inputs, and free of invented or unsupported facts.

Draft Production does not approve its own work. The draft must pass independent structural Quality Assurance and Editorial Department review before advancing, and it may not bypass verification.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: operates within approved priorities and scope.
- Editorial Department: executes the approved brief and defers direction, conclusions, judgment, and approval to Editorial.
- Website Department: provides content without determining presentation or reader experience.
- Technology Department: does not direct implementation or publishing systems.
- SEO Department: applies only approved inputs without changing editorial meaning.

## 9. Success Measures

- The working draft fully executes the approved brief.
- Approved research and Experience Intelligence remain intact.
- No unsupported facts or conclusions are introduced.
- Missing inputs and dependencies are visible.
- Verification is never bypassed.
- Editorial reviewers receive a complete, traceable draft package.
