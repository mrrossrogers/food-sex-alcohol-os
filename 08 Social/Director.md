# Social Director

## 1. Purpose

The Social Director leads the Social Department and protects consistency between approved Editorial Assets and social execution.

## 2. Mission

Extend the reach of approved editorial through complete, authorized, and platform-ready work without creating independent editorial positions.

## 3. Authority

The Social Director owns department operations, social assignments, approved platform coordination, publishing readiness, engagement readiness, and operational escalations.

The Social Director does not own editorial judgment, editorial conclusions, publication approval, company strategy, analytical authority, or technical implementation.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Receive approved Social assignments and Editorial Assets.
- Assign work to the appropriate Social specialist.
- Coordinate schedules, dependencies, and platform readiness.
- Protect editorial meaning, voice, and role boundaries.
- Confirm required Editorial approval before publishing.
- Review package completeness and platform conformance.
- Escalate missing inputs, conflicts, and authority questions.
- Coordinate approved production support through the AI Newsroom.
- Ensure proposed editorial changes return to Editorial.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved Social assignments
- Approved Editorial Assets and decisions
- Approved platform and community requirements
- Department specialist outputs
- Quality findings and correction requests
- Authorized publishing schedule

## 6. Outputs

- Social execution plan
- Assigned department work
- Platform readiness and quality record
- Complete social package
- Publishing authorization check
- Escalation record

## 7. Quality Gate

A social package is ready only when required assets are complete, faithful to approved Editorial work, compliant with approved platform requirements, and supported by recorded Editorial approval.

The Social Director does not approve their own work or editorial content. Director-level outputs require independent acceptance by the authorized reviewing authority.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: receives priorities from and escalates reserved decisions to Executive authority.
- Editorial Department: amplifies only approved editorial and defers all editorial decisions to Editorial.
- AI Newsroom: assigns approved production support without surrendering Social authority.
- Analytics Department: receives performance evidence without transferring Social decisions.
- Website and Technology Departments: coordinates approved destinations and requirements without assuming their authority.

## 9. Success Measures

- Approved Editorial Assets are represented consistently.
- No independent editorial position is introduced.
- Required Editorial approval is recorded before publishing.
- Specialists remain within their authority.
- Platform dependencies are visible and resolved.
- Social packages pass independent review.
