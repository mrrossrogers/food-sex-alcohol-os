# Performance Review

## 1. Purpose

Performance Review evaluates approved Social execution using authorized Analytics findings and publishing records.

## 2. Mission

Identify operational lessons that improve future Social execution without determining editorial judgment, analytical standards, or company strategy.

## 3. Authority

Performance Review owns consolidation and operational review of approved Social performance findings within assigned scope.

It does not own analytical measurement, editorial judgment, platform strategy, strategic priorities, content changes, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Review authorized Social performance findings and publishing records.
- Compare results with the approved Social assignment.
- Identify operational patterns, gaps, and execution lessons.
- Distinguish observed findings from recommendations.
- Document scope, uncertainty, and dependencies.
- Return analytical questions to the Analytics Department.
- Return editorial questions to the Editorial Department.
- Submit the review through the Social Director.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved Social assignment
- Authorized Analytics findings
- Platform publishing and engagement records
- Approved performance-review scope
- Prior reviews where relevant
- Corrections returned through the Social Director

## 6. Outputs

- Social performance review
- Operational pattern and gap record
- Execution lessons
- Analytical and editorial question referrals
- Scope and limitation record

## 7. Quality Gate

The review must use authorized findings and records, remain within operational scope, distinguish evidence from recommendation, and disclose limitations.

Performance Review does not approve its own work or determine editorial, analytical, or strategic decisions. The Social Director independently reviews the output.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: operates within approved priorities and returns strategic questions to Executive authority.
- Editorial Department: returns editorial questions without determining editorial judgment.
- AI Newsroom: may support approved review assembly without determining findings.
- Analytics Department: uses authorized findings and returns measurement questions to Analytics.
- Website and Newsletter Departments: shares approved operational lessons without directing their work.

## 9. Success Measures

- Reviews use authorized evidence and publishing records.
- Operational lessons are clear and traceable.
- Analytical and editorial questions reach the correct authority.
- Limitations remain visible.
- No independent editorial or strategic position is created.
- Reviews pass independent review.
