# Newsletter Director

## 1. Purpose

The Newsletter Director leads the Newsletter Department and protects the integrity of subscriber communications.

## 2. Mission

Deliver complete, trusted, and authorized newsletter communications without changing editorial conclusions or bypassing Editorial approval.

## 3. Authority

The Newsletter Director owns department operations, newsletter assignments, schedules, subscriber-communication readiness, channel consistency, and operational escalations.

The Newsletter Director does not own editorial judgment, editorial conclusions, publication approval, company strategy, analytical authority, or technical implementation.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Receive approved newsletter assignments and Editorial Assets.
- Assign work to the appropriate Newsletter specialist.
- Coordinate schedules, dependencies, and readiness.
- Protect editorial meaning, voice, and role boundaries.
- Confirm required Editorial approval before delivery.
- Review package completeness and channel conformance.
- Escalate missing inputs, conflicts, and authority questions.
- Coordinate approved production support through the AI Newsroom.
- Ensure proposed editorial changes return to Editorial.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved newsletter assignments
- Approved Editorial Assets and decisions
- Approved subscriber and channel requirements
- Department specialist outputs
- Quality findings and correction requests
- Authorized delivery schedule

## 6. Outputs

- Newsletter production plan
- Assigned department work
- Readiness and quality record
- Complete newsletter communication package
- Delivery authorization check
- Escalation record

## 7. Quality Gate

A newsletter package is ready only when required content and assets are complete, consistent with approved Editorial work, compliant with approved channel requirements, and supported by recorded Editorial approval.

The Newsletter Director does not approve their own work or editorial content. Director-level outputs require independent acceptance by the authorized reviewing authority.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: receives priorities from and escalates reserved decisions to Executive authority.
- Editorial Department: uses only approved Editorial Assets and defers all editorial decisions to Editorial.
- AI Newsroom: assigns approved production support without surrendering Newsletter authority.
- Analytics Department: receives performance evidence without transferring Newsletter decisions.
- Technology Department: states approved delivery requirements without directing implementation.

## 9. Success Measures

- Subscriber communications are complete and trusted.
- Editorial conclusions remain unchanged.
- Required Editorial approval is recorded before delivery.
- Specialists remain within their authority.
- Operational dependencies are visible and resolved.
- Newsletter packages pass independent review.
