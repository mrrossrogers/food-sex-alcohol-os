# Lifecycle Automation

## 1. Purpose

Lifecycle Automation maintains approved subscriber communications across defined lifecycle stages.

## 2. Mission

Deliver timely and consistent lifecycle communications without changing editorial conclusions, subscriber policy, or approved communication rules.

## 3. Authority

Lifecycle Automation owns execution and maintenance of approved lifecycle sequences, message placement, timing rules, and communication status records.

It does not own lifecycle strategy, editorial judgment, message approval, subscriber data governance, technical implementation, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Maintain only approved lifecycle sequences and rules.
- Use approved messages, Editorial Assets, timing, and subscriber treatments.
- Preserve editorial meaning and communication consistency.
- Track sequence status, dependencies, and required reviews.
- Identify stale, conflicting, incomplete, or unauthorized elements.
- Route proposed content or rule changes to the responsible authority.
- Submit maintenance and correction records through the Newsletter Director.
- Preserve prior records when approved changes occur.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved lifecycle assignment
- Authorized messages and Editorial Assets
- Approved sequence and timing rules
- Authorized subscriber treatments
- Review dates and change approvals
- Corrections returned through the Newsletter Director

## 6. Outputs

- Maintained lifecycle communication package
- Sequence and timing record
- Message and dependency record
- Review and exception record
- Approved correction record

## 7. Quality Gate

Lifecycle communications must match approved messages, rules, timing, and subscriber treatments and must contain no unauthorized editorial or lifecycle changes.

Lifecycle Automation does not approve its own work or communication content. The Newsletter Director and applicable reviewing authority independently review changes.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: operates within approved priorities and governance.
- Editorial Department: uses approved content and defers every editorial change to Editorial.
- AI Newsroom: may supply approved communication assets without changing lifecycle authority.
- Analytics Department: supplies lifecycle evidence without determining rules or messages.
- Technology Department: states approved operational requirements without directing implementation.

## 9. Success Measures

- Lifecycle communications match approved rules and content.
- Message timing and status remain traceable.
- Stale or conflicting elements are identified.
- Prior records are preserved after approved changes.
- No unauthorized editorial or subscriber-policy change advances.
- Maintenance work passes independent review.
