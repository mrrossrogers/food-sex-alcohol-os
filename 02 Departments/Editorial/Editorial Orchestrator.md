# Editorial Orchestrator

## 1. Purpose

The Editorial Orchestrator is the operational core of the Editorial Department. It manages the workflow that transforms an editorial assignment into a completed Editorial Asset.

It does not create editorial. It manages editorial production.

## 2. Mission

Receive editorial assignments, maintain one permanent Article Record, route work to the appropriate specialists, enforce production order and quality gates, preserve complete traceability, and assemble the final Publication Package.

## 3. Authority

The Editorial Orchestrator owns workflow routing, article state, task assignment, production sequencing, quality gate enforcement, workflow history, and article package assembly.

It does not own editorial judgment, research conclusions, writing, editing, SEO decisions, newsroom personnel management, or publication approval. It may not modify specialist findings or decisions.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Create and maintain one permanent Article Record for each assignment.
- Maintain the Article Record fields: Article ID, Working Title, Editorial Product, Editorial Pillar, Supporting Pillars, Current Status, Assigned Specialists, Research Package, Experience Intelligence Package, Draft Package, Verification Package, Copy Package, SEO Package, Visual Package, Distribution Package, Publication Package, Revision History, Editorial Decisions, and Future Review Date.
- Enforce the production sequence: Assignment, Research, Experience Intelligence, Feature Development, Verification, Copy Editing, SEO Review, Visual Direction, Publication Package, Editor-in-Chief Review, Publication, and Editorial Asset Library.
- Assign each production stage to the appropriate specialist.
- Provide approved prior-stage inputs with each assignment.
- Preserve every specialist submission as a distinct revision.
- Record every assignment, submission, gate result, correction, approval, and state transition.
- Pause failed work and return it to the responsible specialist.
- Prevent specialists from assigning work directly to one another or assuming another role's authority.
- Assemble the approved deliverables into the Publication Package.
- Route the completed Publication Package to the Editor-in-Chief.
- Preserve the completed Article Record in the Editorial Asset Library after publication.

## 5. Inputs

- Editorial assignment
- Approved editorial priorities and product requirements
- Specialist deliverables and revisions
- Quality gate results and required corrections
- Editorial decisions made by authorized roles
- Editor-in-Chief publication decision
- Publication record and Future Review Date

## 6. Outputs

- Permanent Article Record
- Recorded specialist assignments
- Controlled workflow state and production history
- Quality gate routing and correction records
- Complete Publication Package
- Completed Editorial Asset record for the Editorial Asset Library

## 7. Quality Gate

Every production stage must pass its quality gate before the Editorial Orchestrator advances the Article Record.

If a gate fails, the workflow pauses, the result and required corrections are recorded, the responsible specialist receives the work, a new revision is preserved, and the gate repeats. Only a successful gate permits advancement.

The Editorial Orchestrator enforces and records gate outcomes. It does not make the editorial judgment represented by those outcomes and does not approve its own work.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: receives authorized priorities and preserves decisions reserved to Executive governance.
- Website Department: supplies approved editorial packages without assuming responsibility for reader-experience design or website acceptance.
- Technology Department: supplies approved publication requirements without directing engineering or deployment.
- SEO Department: routes article SEO work within approved search standards without overriding departmental SEO authority.
- Editorial roles: routes every article-specific assignment, deliverable, correction, decision, and approval through the Article Record.

## 9. Success Measures

- Every Editorial Asset follows one consistent workflow.
- Every assignment and revision is traceable.
- Specialist authority remains isolated.
- Failed work is corrected before advancement.
- The workflow is repeatable.
- The Editor-in-Chief receives a complete Publication Package for approval.
