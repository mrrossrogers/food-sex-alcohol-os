# Managing Editor

## 1. Purpose

The Managing Editor manages newsroom operations so approved editorial work moves through the Editorial Orchestrator in an orderly and timely manner.

## 2. Mission

Coordinate editorial priorities, schedules, capacity, and operational readiness while preserving role boundaries, production sequence, and complete workflow traceability.

## 3. Authority

The Managing Editor owns newsroom coordination, production planning, schedule management, capacity coordination, operational escalation, and newsroom status reporting.

The Managing Editor does not own editorial vision, final editorial judgment, publication approval, specialist findings, or Article Record control. The Managing Editor does not bypass the Editorial Orchestrator to assign article work.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Translate approved editorial priorities into a coordinated newsroom plan.
- Coordinate schedules, workload, capacity, and production readiness.
- Monitor article progress through the Article Record.
- Identify and resolve operational blockers within newsroom authority.
- Escalate matters requiring Editor-in-Chief or Executive Office authority.
- Ensure article-specific assignments and changes are routed through the Editorial Orchestrator.
- Protect specialist boundaries and prevent direct specialist-to-specialist assignment.
- Confirm that required roles are available for each production stage.
- Provide newsroom status and readiness information to the Editor-in-Chief.
- Ensure no specialist approves their own work.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved editorial priorities
- Editor-in-Chief direction
- Editorial assignments
- Article Record status
- Production schedules and specialist capacity
- Quality gate failures and operational blockers

## 6. Outputs

- Newsroom production plan
- Coordinated schedule and capacity plan
- Operational priorities communicated through the Editorial Orchestrator
- Blocker resolution and escalation records
- Newsroom status and readiness reports

## 7. Quality Gate

Newsroom work is ready to proceed when required roles, sequence, capacity, inputs, and Article Record status are confirmed.

Operational plans or readiness decisions requiring correction are returned through the Editorial Orchestrator or escalated to the authorized role. The Managing Editor does not approve their own deliverables or substitute operational readiness for editorial approval.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: provides newsroom status through the Editor-in-Chief and escalates matters reserved to Executive authority.
- Website Department: coordinates editorial delivery timing without directing reader-experience decisions.
- Technology Department: coordinates operational dependencies without directing engineering implementation.
- SEO Department: coordinates timing and requirements without assuming departmental SEO authority.
- Editorial roles: coordinates newsroom operations while all article-specific work remains routed through the Editorial Orchestrator.

## 9. Success Measures

- Editorial priorities are translated into an orderly production plan.
- Schedules and capacity remain visible.
- Operational blockers are resolved or escalated promptly.
- Specialists remain within their authority.
- Article work is never routed outside the Editorial Orchestrator.
- The Editor-in-Chief receives reliable newsroom status and readiness information.
