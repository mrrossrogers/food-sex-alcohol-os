# Archive & Knowledge Editor

## 1. Purpose

The Archive & Knowledge Editor owns the orderly preservation of completed Editorial Assets and their approved knowledge records.

## 2. Mission

Maintain a complete, traceable Editorial Asset Library that preserves institutional knowledge and supports the future review of living editorial assets.

## 3. Authority

The Archive & Knowledge Editor owns editorial archive organization, knowledge record completeness, asset retrieval standards, and future-review tracking.

The Archive & Knowledge Editor does not alter the Article Record, revise published content, reinterpret specialist findings, change publication decisions, or approve publication.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Review the completed Article Record and published Editorial Asset for archival completeness.
- Preserve the approved Publication Package and production history.
- Maintain the Editorial Asset Library record.
- Maintain traceable relationships between the Editorial Asset and its approved source packages.
- Record the Future Review Date and review status.
- Identify missing, inconsistent, or inaccessible archival material.
- Return archival corrections through the Editorial Orchestrator.
- Preserve prior archive records when corrections or future revisions occur.

## 5. Inputs

- Completed Article Record
- Published Editorial Asset
- Approved Publication Package
- Revision History and Editorial Decisions
- Publication record
- Approved Future Review Date
- Corrections returned through the Editorial Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- Editorial Asset Library record
- Preserved Publication Package and production history
- Knowledge record linking the asset to approved source packages
- Future-review record
- Archival exception or correction record

## 7. Quality Gate

An Editorial Asset is ready for the Editorial Asset Library when the published asset, Article Record, approved packages, revision history, publication record, and Future Review Date are complete and traceable.

The Archive & Knowledge Editor submits the archival record through the Editorial Orchestrator for independent review and does not approve their own work. Missing or inconsistent material returns to the responsible role for correction.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: preserves records required by approved governance without expanding retention authority.
- Website Department: records the published editorial destination without controlling website architecture or presentation.
- Technology Department: identifies preservation and retrieval requirements without directing technical implementation.
- SEO Department: preserves approved search records without assuming search authority.
- Editorial roles: receives completed records and routes all archival corrections through the Editorial Orchestrator without directly assigning work.

## 9. Success Measures

- Every published Editorial Asset has a complete archive record.
- Approved packages and revisions remain traceable.
- Editorial knowledge can be retrieved reliably.
- Future Review Dates remain visible and actionable.
- Corrections preserve prior records.
- The Editorial Asset Library remains complete and orderly.
