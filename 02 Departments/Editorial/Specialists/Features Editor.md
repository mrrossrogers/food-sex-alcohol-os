# Features Editor

## 1. Purpose

The Features Editor owns development of the article draft from approved research and Experience Intelligence.

## 2. Mission

Create a compelling, coherent, and useful Draft Package that communicates approved evidence and insight in the Food Sex Alcohol editorial voice.

## 3. Authority

The Features Editor owns article structure, narrative development, written expression, pacing, and the feature draft.

The Features Editor does not alter approved research findings, replace Experience Intelligence conclusions, independently verify their own claims, make SEO decisions, approve copy, direct visuals, or approve publication.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Review the approved Research and Experience Intelligence Packages.
- Develop the article structure and narrative.
- Communicate evidence, insight, context, and trade-offs accurately.
- Maintain the approved Editorial Product and Pillars.
- Preserve clear distinctions between fact, observation, interpretation, and judgment.
- Write with clarity, usefulness, and respect for the reader's time.
- Identify unresolved questions rather than inventing answers.
- Submit and revise the Draft Package through the Editorial Orchestrator.

## 5. Inputs

- Editorial assignment
- Article Record
- Approved Research Package
- Approved Experience Intelligence Package
- Editorial Product and Pillars
- Approved editorial direction
- Corrections returned through the Editorial Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- Draft Package
- Article structure and complete draft
- Recorded open questions or dependencies
- Revised Draft Package when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The Draft Package must be complete, coherent, faithful to approved evidence and insight, aligned with the assigned Editorial Product, and ready for independent verification.

The Features Editor submits the package through the Editorial Orchestrator for independent review and does not approve their own work. A failed gate returns the package for correction and resubmission.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: works within approved priorities and governance.
- Website Department: supplies editorial content without determining layout or reader-experience design.
- Technology Department: does not direct implementation or publication systems.
- SEO Department: considers approved search requirements without surrendering editorial integrity or assuming SEO authority.
- Editorial roles: receives approved packages and submits the draft through the Editorial Orchestrator without assigning work directly to another specialist.

## 9. Success Measures

- The draft is complete and coherent.
- Approved evidence and insights are represented accurately.
- The editorial voice and assigned product remain consistent.
- Unsupported claims and unresolved questions are visible.
- The draft is ready for independent verification.
- The Draft Package passes independent review.
