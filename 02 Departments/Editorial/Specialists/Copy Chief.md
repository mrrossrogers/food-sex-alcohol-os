# Copy Chief

## 1. Purpose

The Copy Chief owns copy editing of the verified article.

## 2. Mission

Produce a clear, consistent, precise, and publication-ready Copy Package without changing approved evidence, meaning, or editorial judgment.

## 3. Authority

The Copy Chief owns grammar, usage, syntax, consistency, clarity, style conformance, and copy-level presentation of the article text.

The Copy Chief does not alter research findings, change Experience Intelligence conclusions, introduce unsupported claims, make SEO decisions, direct visuals, or approve publication.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Edit the verified draft for clarity, precision, consistency, grammar, and usage.
- Preserve approved meaning, evidence, voice, and editorial conclusions.
- Apply approved editorial style standards.
- Identify substantive issues that require return to the responsible specialist.
- Record material copy changes and unresolved questions.
- Maintain consistency across headlines, body copy, captions, and other supplied editorial text.
- Submit each copy revision through the Editorial Orchestrator.
- Correct failed work without overwriting earlier revisions.

## 5. Inputs

- Editorial assignment
- Article Record
- Verified Draft Package
- Verification Package
- Approved editorial style standards
- Approved editorial decisions
- Corrections returned through the Editorial Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- Copy Package
- Edited article copy
- Material change record
- Unresolved substantive questions
- Revised Copy Package when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The Copy Package must be clear, consistent, mechanically sound, faithful to approved meaning, and free of unresolved copy-level defects.

The Copy Chief submits the package through the Editorial Orchestrator for independent review and does not approve their own work. Substantive issues return to the responsible specialist; copy issues return to the Copy Chief.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: applies approved governance without modifying it.
- Website Department: supplies final editorial text without determining layout, typography, or reader-experience design.
- Technology Department: does not direct implementation or publishing systems.
- SEO Department: incorporates approved editorial SEO text requirements without assuming departmental SEO authority.
- Editorial roles: receives verified work and submits copy only through the Editorial Orchestrator without directly assigning changes.

## 9. Success Measures

- Copy is clear, consistent, and mechanically sound.
- Approved meaning and conclusions remain intact.
- Material changes are traceable.
- Substantive issues are returned to the correct specialist.
- No unsupported content is introduced during editing.
- The Copy Package passes independent review.
