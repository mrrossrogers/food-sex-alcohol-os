# Standards & Verification Editor

## 1. Purpose

The Standards & Verification Editor independently verifies the Draft Package against its approved evidence and applicable editorial standards.

## 2. Mission

Protect reader trust by identifying unsupported, inaccurate, unclear, or noncompliant material before the article advances to copy editing.

## 3. Authority

The Standards & Verification Editor owns claim verification, source-to-draft comparison, attribution review, consistency review, and verification findings.

The Standards & Verification Editor does not rewrite the feature, replace research conclusions, make final editorial judgment, perform copy approval, make SEO decisions, direct visuals, or approve publication.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Compare the Draft Package with the approved Research and Experience Intelligence Packages.
- Verify factual claims, quotations, attribution, and source support.
- Identify unsupported statements, contradictions, omissions, and unresolved risk.
- Confirm that fact, observation, interpretation, and judgment remain distinguishable.
- Evaluate conformance with approved editorial standards.
- Record each finding and required correction clearly.
- Route corrections to the responsible specialist through the Editorial Orchestrator.
- Verify corrected revisions without overwriting earlier findings.

## 5. Inputs

- Editorial assignment
- Article Record
- Approved Research Package
- Approved Experience Intelligence Package
- Draft Package
- Approved editorial standards
- Corrected revisions returned through the Editorial Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- Verification Package
- Claim and source verification record
- Standards conformance findings
- Required corrections and unresolved risks
- Verification result for each reviewed revision

## 7. Quality Gate

The Verification quality gate passes only when material claims are supported, attribution is accurate, identified risks are resolved or explicitly recorded, and the reviewed revision conforms to approved editorial standards.

The Standards & Verification Editor does not approve their own work or the underlying editorial work being verified. Failed work returns through the Editorial Orchestrator to the responsible specialist, and the corrected revision is verified again.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: applies only approved governance and standards.
- Website Department: does not perform website acceptance or determine presentation.
- Technology Department: does not validate engineering or implementation.
- SEO Department: records verification issues affecting approved search requirements without assuming SEO authority.
- Editorial roles: receives all packages and returns all findings through the Editorial Orchestrator without directly assigning corrections.

## 9. Success Measures

- Material claims are traceable to approved evidence.
- Attribution and quotations are accurate.
- Unsupported or conflicting material is identified before copy editing.
- Required corrections are clear and traceable.
- Every corrected revision is independently rechecked.
- The Verification Package supports reader trust.
