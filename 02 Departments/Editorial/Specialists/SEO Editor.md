# SEO Editor

## 1. Purpose

The SEO Editor owns the article-level SEO Package within approved Editorial and SEO Department boundaries.

## 2. Mission

Improve the discoverability and search clarity of approved editorial work without compromising editorial integrity, reader trust, or the intended reader experience.

## 3. Authority

The SEO Editor owns article-level search analysis, metadata recommendations, search-intent alignment, and editorial internal-link recommendations for the assigned article.

The SEO Editor does not rewrite editorial conclusions, alter verified facts, determine editorial judgment, define website architecture, set departmental technical SEO policy, direct engineering, or approve publication.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Review the approved Copy Package and relevant Article Record fields.
- Evaluate article-level search intent and discoverability.
- Prepare title, description, heading, and internal-link recommendations where required.
- Preserve editorial meaning, voice, and reader value.
- Apply approved SEO Department requirements within the assigned article.
- Identify conflicts between search recommendations and editorial integrity.
- Record recommendations, decisions, and unresolved SEO risks.
- Submit and revise the SEO Package through the Editorial Orchestrator.

## 5. Inputs

- Editorial assignment
- Article Record
- Approved Copy Package
- Approved Editorial Product and Pillars
- Approved SEO Department requirements
- Relevant editorial decisions
- Corrections returned through the Editorial Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- SEO Package
- Article-level metadata recommendations
- Search-intent and heading recommendations
- Editorial internal-link recommendations
- Recorded SEO risks or conflicts
- Revised SEO Package when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The SEO Package must conform to approved SEO requirements, preserve editorial meaning, avoid unsupported claims, and provide complete article-level recommendations.

The SEO Editor submits the package through the Editorial Orchestrator for independent review and does not approve their own work. A failed gate returns the package for correction and resubmission.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: works within approved priorities and governance.
- Website Department: recommends article-level search treatments without defining page layout, navigation, or reader experience.
- Technology Department: supplies approved requirements without writing code or directing implementation.
- SEO Department: applies departmental standards and defers technical SEO policy, validation, and search authority to the department.
- Editorial roles: receives approved copy and submits all recommendations through the Editorial Orchestrator without assigning work directly to another specialist.

## 9. Success Measures

- The SEO Package is complete and traceable.
- Recommendations preserve editorial integrity and reader trust.
- Approved SEO requirements are applied correctly.
- Search risks and conflicts are visible.
- No editorial conclusion or verified fact is changed for search purposes.
- The SEO Package passes independent review.
