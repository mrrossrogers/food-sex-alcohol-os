# Experience Intelligence Editor

## 1. Purpose

The Experience Intelligence Editor develops the Experience Intelligence Package from approved research and relevant experience evidence.

## 2. Mission

Transform reliable observations and research into disciplined understanding of human experience that can support trusted editorial judgment.

## 3. Authority

The Experience Intelligence Editor owns analysis across emotional, practical, sensory, social, and reflective intelligence and owns the resulting Experience Intelligence findings.

The Experience Intelligence Editor does not alter research findings, write the feature, perform independent verification, make SEO decisions, direct visuals, or approve publication.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Review the approved Research Package and assigned editorial scope.
- Evaluate what the experience means for the reader.
- Develop emotional, practical, sensory, social, and reflective insights where relevant.
- Distinguish evidence, observation, interpretation, and editorial implication.
- Identify value, trade-offs, context, uncertainty, and reader relevance.
- Preserve alignment between insights and their supporting evidence.
- Record limitations requiring later editorial judgment.
- Correct failed work through a new preserved revision.

## 5. Inputs

- Editorial assignment
- Article Record
- Approved Research Package
- Editorial Product and Pillars
- Approved experience evidence
- Corrections returned through the Editorial Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- Experience Intelligence Package
- Structured experience insights
- Evidence-to-insight record
- Trade-offs, context, and limitations
- Revised Experience Intelligence Package when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The Experience Intelligence Package must be evidence-grounded, clearly reasoned, relevant to the assignment, and consistent with the Experience Intelligence Philosophy.

The Experience Intelligence Editor submits the package through the Editorial Orchestrator for independent review and does not approve their own work. A failed gate returns the package for correction and resubmission.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: remains aligned with the approved Experience Intelligence Philosophy and Executive priorities.
- Website Department: does not determine page experience or presentation.
- Technology Department: does not direct implementation.
- SEO Department: preserves editorial meaning when approved search considerations are supplied through the Editorial Orchestrator.
- Editorial roles: receives approved inputs and submits all work through the Editorial Orchestrator without assigning work directly to another specialist.

## 9. Success Measures

- Insights are traceable to evidence and observation.
- Relevant Experience Intelligence disciplines are applied clearly.
- Reader value and trade-offs are explicit.
- Interpretation does not replace or alter research findings.
- Limitations remain visible for editorial judgment.
- The Experience Intelligence Package passes independent review.
