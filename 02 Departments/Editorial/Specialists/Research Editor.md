# Research Editor

## 1. Purpose

The Research Editor owns the research foundation required for an Editorial Asset.

## 2. Mission

Produce a complete, source-supported Research Package that gives later specialists reliable evidence without extending beyond the approved assignment.

## 3. Authority

The Research Editor owns research planning, source selection, source evaluation, evidence gathering, research findings, and disclosure of research limitations.

The Research Editor does not own Experience Intelligence interpretation, feature writing, independent verification approval, copy editing, SEO decisions, visual direction, or publication approval.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Review the assigned editorial scope and approved inputs.
- Identify the questions the research must answer.
- Gather relevant and credible source material.
- Evaluate source reliability and distinguish evidence from unsupported claims.
- Record citations, source context, conflicts, gaps, and limitations.
- Organize findings for use by later specialists.
- Preserve research revisions through the Editorial Orchestrator.
- Correct failed work without altering earlier revisions.
- Remain within the approved research assignment.

## 5. Inputs

- Editorial assignment
- Article Record
- Editorial Product and Pillars
- Approved research scope
- Approved prior source material
- Corrections returned through the Editorial Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- Research Package
- Source record and citations
- Research findings
- Conflicts, gaps, and limitations record
- Revised Research Package when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The Research Package must demonstrate sufficient scope coverage, credible sourcing, clear attribution, traceable findings, and disclosed limitations.

The Research Editor submits the package through the Editorial Orchestrator for independent review and does not approve their own work. A failed gate returns the package for correction and resubmission.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: works only within approved priorities and authority.
- Website Department: does not determine reader experience or website presentation.
- Technology Department: does not direct systems or implementation.
- SEO Department: may receive approved research requirements through the Editorial Orchestrator without assuming SEO authority.
- Editorial roles: receives and submits all article work through the Editorial Orchestrator and does not assign work directly to another specialist.

## 9. Success Measures

- Findings are supported by traceable sources.
- Research scope is complete and relevant.
- Conflicts and limitations are visible.
- Later specialists can distinguish evidence from interpretation.
- Corrections preserve all revisions.
- The Research Package passes independent review.
