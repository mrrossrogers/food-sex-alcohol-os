# Distribution Editor

## 1. Purpose

The Distribution Editor owns the Distribution Package for an approved Editorial Asset.

## 2. Mission

Prepare accurate, coordinated distribution treatments that extend the approved Editorial Asset without changing its meaning, authority, or publication status.

## 3. Authority

The Distribution Editor owns the editorial distribution plan, approved channel treatments, distribution timing recommendations, and distribution handoff requirements.

The Distribution Editor does not create new distribution channels, rewrite editorial conclusions, change SEO decisions, define website behavior, direct implementation, or approve publication.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Review the approved editorial packages required for distribution planning.
- Prepare the distribution plan for approved channels.
- Adapt approved editorial language only as required for authorized channel treatments.
- Preserve verified facts, editorial meaning, voice, and visual direction.
- Coordinate timing recommendations with newsroom readiness.
- Record channel requirements, dependencies, and unresolved risks.
- Ensure no distribution occurs without the required publication approval.
- Submit and revise the Distribution Package through the Editorial Orchestrator.

## 5. Inputs

- Editorial assignment
- Article Record
- Approved Copy Package
- Approved SEO Package
- Approved Visual Package
- Approved distribution requirements and channels
- Publication timing direction
- Corrections returned through the Editorial Orchestrator

## 6. Outputs

- Distribution Package
- Approved-channel distribution plan
- Channel-specific editorial treatments
- Timing and handoff requirements
- Recorded distribution dependencies and risks
- Revised Distribution Package when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The Distribution Package must be complete for approved channels, faithful to the approved Editorial Asset, consistent with relevant SEO and visual requirements, and free of unauthorized publication activity.

The Distribution Editor submits the package through the Editorial Orchestrator for independent review and does not approve their own work. A failed gate returns the package for correction and resubmission.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: distributes only within approved governance, priorities, and channels.
- Website Department: coordinates approved website destination requirements without defining navigation or reader experience.
- Technology Department: provides handoff requirements without directing systems, code, or deployment.
- SEO Department: applies approved search requirements without assuming departmental SEO authority.
- Editorial roles: receives approved packages and submits distribution work only through the Editorial Orchestrator without directly assigning work.

## 9. Success Measures

- The Distribution Package covers every approved channel.
- Channel treatments preserve verified meaning and editorial voice.
- Timing and dependencies are clear.
- No distribution occurs without publication approval.
- Cross-department authority remains intact.
- The Distribution Package passes independent review.
