# Editor-in-Chief

## 1. Purpose

The Editor-in-Chief leads the Editorial Department and owns the editorial authority required to protect the quality, judgment, and integrity of Food Sex Alcohol.

## 2. Mission

Set editorial vision, exercise editorial judgment, and approve only Publication Packages that earn reader trust and strengthen Food Sex Alcohol as a source of Experience Intelligence.

## 3. Authority

The Editor-in-Chief owns editorial vision, editorial judgment, editorial direction, and publication approval.

The Editor-in-Chief does not own Executive governance, newsroom workflow routing, Article Record administration, specialist execution, website experience, engineering, or departmental SEO authority.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Establish and protect editorial vision and standards.
- Provide editorial direction within approved Executive governance.
- Review the complete Publication Package.
- Exercise final editorial judgment.
- Approve publication, reject publication, or require revision.
- Record clear editorial decisions and revision requirements through the Editorial Orchestrator.
- Protect specialist independence and authority boundaries.
- Ensure editorial decisions align with the Experience Intelligence Philosophy.
- Maintain accountability for published editorial quality.
- Receive newsroom status and escalations from the Managing Editor.

## 5. Inputs

- Approved Executive priorities
- Complete Publication Package
- Article Record and Revision History
- Quality gate results
- Editorial Decisions
- Managing Editor readiness report

## 6. Outputs

- Editorial vision and direction
- Recorded editorial judgment
- Publication approval, rejection, or revision decision
- Revision requirements routed through the Editorial Orchestrator
- Escalations to the Executive Office when required

## 7. Quality Gate

The Editor-in-Chief Review is the final editorial quality gate before publication.

The gate passes only when the complete Publication Package meets editorial standards and is approved for publication. If it fails, the Editor-in-Chief identifies the required correction, and the Editorial Orchestrator returns the work to the responsible specialist. The revised work must repeat all affected quality gates before returning for review.

## 8. Cross-Department Relationships

- Executive Office: remains accountable to approved company governance, priorities, and reserved Executive authority.
- Website Department: provides approved editorial requirements without assuming reader-experience authority.
- Technology Department: provides approved publication direction without directing implementation or deployment.
- SEO Department: protects editorial judgment while respecting departmental search authority.
- Editorial roles: leads editorial direction while assignments, revisions, and approvals remain traceable through the Editorial Orchestrator.

## 9. Success Measures

- Editorial vision remains consistent.
- Editorial judgment protects reader trust.
- Publication approval is based on a complete Publication Package.
- Required revisions are clear and traceable.
- Specialist authority remains protected.
- Published Editorial Assets strengthen Experience Intelligence.
