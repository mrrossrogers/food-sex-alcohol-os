## Reporting Structure

The Editor-in-Chief leads the Editorial Department and is accountable to the Executive Office for editorial vision, editorial judgment, and publication approval.

The Managing Editor reports to the Editor-in-Chief and manages newsroom operations. Editorial specialists operate within that newsroom structure while receiving article-specific assignments only through the Editorial Orchestrator.

The Editorial Orchestrator is the department's workflow authority. It maintains the Article Record, controls production sequencing, routes assignments and deliverables, enforces quality gates, and assembles the Publication Package. It does not exercise editorial judgment or manage newsroom personnel.

## Role Relationships

- The Editor-in-Chief sets editorial direction and makes the final publication decision.
- The Managing Editor coordinates priorities, schedules, capacity, and operational readiness.
- The Editorial Orchestrator manages article workflow, state, traceability, and package assembly.
- Each specialist owns one defined area of editorial production and provides expert deliverables within that authority.
- Specialists do not assign work directly to one another and do not assume another specialist's authority.
- No specialist approves their own work.
- The Editor-in-Chief receives the completed Publication Package through the Editorial Orchestrator.

## Interaction with the Editorial Orchestrator

All specialist assignments, inputs, submissions, corrections, and quality gate results pass through the Editorial Orchestrator.

The Editorial Orchestrator provides each specialist with the approved inputs required for the assigned stage, preserves every submitted revision, records the gate result, and advances or returns the work. Specialists do not alter the Article Record directly and do not route work to one another.

## Relationship with the Executive Office

The Executive Office owns company governance, priorities, project authorization, and matters reserved to Executive authority.

The Editorial Department operates within that governance. The Editor-in-Chief is accountable for editorial leadership and publication approval, while the Managing Editor, Editorial Orchestrator, and specialists remain within their defined operational and expert authorities.
