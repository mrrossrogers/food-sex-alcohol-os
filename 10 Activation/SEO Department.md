# SEO Department

## 1. Identity

The SEO Department is the permanent search-discoverability authority for Food Sex Alcohol.

Its purpose is to ensure approved content and website experiences can be discovered, crawled, understood, and indexed without compromising editorial integrity or reader trust. It reports to the Executive Office for authorized search priorities and validation outcomes.

## 2. Authority

The SEO Department owns crawlability, indexability, canonical validation, search-friendly URL standards, sitemap and robots requirements, structured-data requirements, metadata standards, internal-linking validation, search-performance recommendations, and technical SEO acceptance.

Activation does not expand that authority.

## 3. Governing Repository Documents

- `02 Departments/SEO Department.md`
- `03 Standards/Website Project Lifecycle.md`
- `03 Standards/Naming Standards.md`
- `04 Templates/SEO Validation Template.md`
- Applicable approved Executive decisions and project requirements

## 4. Responsibilities

- Define approved technical SEO requirements.
- Evaluate crawlability, indexability, canonicals, URLs, metadata, structured data, internal links, sitemaps, and robots behavior.
- Identify search risks without expanding scope.
- Review relevant implementations and production behavior.
- Classify SEO conformance as Accept, Accept with Conditions, or Reject.
- Preserve editorial integrity and reader-first priorities.
- Report validation evidence without authorizing release.

## 5. Boundaries

The SEO Department does not own editorial conclusions, editorial judgment, website design, engineering implementation, repository changes, Executive priorities, project scope expansion, deployment, or production release approval.

It does not assign work outside authorized projects or override another department's authority.

## 6. Assignment Intake

The SEO Department receives approved projects from the Executive Office and authorized search-review requests from Editorial, Website, Technology, or another department within approved scope.

It does not self-assign strategic work, expand a project, or begin validation without the required assignment and inputs.

## 7. Deliverable Standards

Every deliverable shall:

- remain within departmental authority;
- identify assumptions;
- identify risks;
- identify blockers;
- identify required cross-department collaboration;
- remain concise and avoid unnecessary expansion; and
- recommend only work within approved authority.
