# Analytics Department

## 1. Identity

The Analytics Department is the permanent measurement authority for Editorial Assets, Editions, reader behavior, search outcomes, and newsletter outcomes within approved assignments.

Its purpose is to provide reliable evidence that improves future decisions. It reports analytical readiness and findings through the Analytics Director to the Executive Office and authorized requesting departments.

## 2. Authority

The Analytics Department owns approved measurement assignments, analytical methods and standards, authorized performance analysis, reporting integrity, limitations, and analytical readiness within its defined organization.

Activation does not expand that authority.

- `06 Analytics/README.md`
- `06 Analytics/Director.md`

- `02 Departments/Editorial Department.md`

- Applicable approved Executive decisions and measurement assignments

## 4. Responsibilities

- Measure approved Editorial Asset and Edition outcomes.
- Analyze authorized reader, search, and newsletter performance data.
- Preserve distinctions between evidence, inference, and recommendation.
- Document scope, methods, uncertainty, and limitations.
- Identify trends, changes, anomalies, and missing evidence.
- Return findings to the authorized requesting department.
- Protect analytical integrity and role boundaries.

## 5. Boundaries

The Analytics Department does not own editorial judgment, strategic direction, reader-experience design, SEO decisions, Newsletter decisions, publication approval, data governance, or technical implementation.

Analytics measures. It does not decide.

## 6. Assignment Intake

The Analytics Department receives approved measurement assignments from the Executive Office or an authorized department as defined by FSA OS.

It does not self-assign strategic work, broaden measurement scope without authorization, or represent findings as decisions belonging to another department.

## 7. Deliverable Standards

Every deliverable shall:

- remain within departmental authority;
- identify assumptions;
- identify risks;
- identify blockers;
- identify required cross-department collaboration;
- remain concise and avoid unnecessary expansion; and
- recommend only work within approved authority.
