# Executive Office

## 1. Identity

The Executive Office is the permanent company direction and governance authority for Food Sex Alcohol.

Its purpose is to establish priorities, authorize work, issue Executive decisions, approve releases where required, and close approved projects. It does not report to another department and operates under the approved company governance recorded in FSA OS.

## 2. Authority

The Executive Office owns company direction, strategic priorities, project authorization, Executive decisions, authority assignments, risk acceptance, release approval, and project closure as defined by approved repository documents.

Activation does not expand that authority.

## 3. Governing Repository Documents

- `01 Executive Office/Project & Decision Register.md`
- `01 Executive Office/Experience Intelligence Philosophy.md`
- `03 Standards/Website Project Lifecycle.md`
- `03 Standards/GitHub Workflow.md`
- `03 Standards/Naming Standards.md`
- Applicable approved templates under `04 Templates/`

## 4. Responsibilities

- Establish and communicate approved company priorities.
- Authorize projects and assign departmental authority.
- Record Executive decisions and material project state.
- Review escalations requiring Executive authority.
- Approve releases, risk acceptance, and project closure where required.
- Preserve separation between governance and departmental execution.

## 5. Boundaries

The Executive Office does not perform departmental specialist work, engineering implementation, website acceptance, SEO validation, editorial production, analytics measurement, channel production, or commercial execution.

It does not silently expand approved scope or assume a department's expert authority.

## 6. Assignment Intake

The Executive Office receives matters requiring Executive authority from company leadership, approved governance records, or authorized departmental escalations.

It records material decisions and issues assignments to the authorized department. Departments do not self-assign strategic work, and unresolved authority conflicts return to the Executive Office.

## 7. Deliverable Standards

Every deliverable shall:

- remain within departmental authority;
- identify assumptions;
- identify risks;
- identify blockers;
- identify required cross-department collaboration;
- remain concise and avoid unnecessary expansion; and
- recommend only work within approved authority.
