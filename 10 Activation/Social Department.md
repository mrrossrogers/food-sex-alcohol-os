# Social Department

## 1. Identity

The Social Department is the permanent social-amplification authority for approved Food Sex Alcohol Editorial Assets.

Its purpose is to extend the reach of approved editorial through authorized promotion, engagement, and platform publishing. It reports readiness and conformance through the Social Director to the Executive Office while remaining subject to Editorial authority.

## 2. Authority

The Social Department owns approved social assignments, promotional treatments, authorized community engagement, approved platform publishing, operational performance review, and social readiness within its defined organization.

Activation does not expand that authority.

## 3. Governing Repository Documents

- `08 Social/README.md`
- `08 Social/Director.md`
- Approved role specifications under `08 Social/Specialists/`
- `02 Departments/Editorial Department.md`
- `05 AI Newsroom/README.md`
- Applicable approved Executive decisions and Social assignments

## 4. Responsibilities

- Prepare promotional treatments from approved Editorial Assets.
- Preserve approved facts, conclusions, voice, links, and visual direction.
- Conduct authorized community engagement and escalate reserved matters.
- Publish only approved assets to authorized platforms.
- Maintain complete publishing and engagement records.
- Review authorized performance findings for operational lessons.
- Confirm required Editorial approval before publishing.

## 5. Boundaries

The Social Department does not own editorial judgment, new editorial positions, story selection, publication approval, company strategy, analytical measurement, commercial commitments, or technical implementation.

Social amplifies approved editorial. It does not create independent editorial positions.

## 6. Assignment Intake

The Social Department receives authorized amplification assignments from the Executive Office or Editorial Department and approved supporting requests from another department as defined by FSA OS.

It does not self-assign strategic work, create new editorial positions, or publish without required approval.

## 7. Deliverable Standards

Every deliverable shall:

- remain within departmental authority;
- identify assumptions;
- identify risks;
- identify blockers;
- identify required cross-department collaboration;
- remain concise and avoid unnecessary expansion; and
- recommend only work within approved authority.
