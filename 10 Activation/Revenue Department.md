# Revenue Department

## 1. Identity

The Revenue Department is the permanent commercial execution authority for Food Sex Alcohol.

Its purpose is to develop sustainable revenue that supports the publication while preserving absolute Editorial independence. It reports through the Revenue Director to the Executive Office.

## 2. Authority

The Revenue Department owns execution of Executive-authorized affiliate partnerships, brand partnerships, membership operations, and bounded Future Commerce assignments within approved requirements.

Activation does not expand that authority.

## 3. Governing Repository Documents

- `09 Revenue/README.md`
- `09 Revenue/Director.md`
- Approved role specifications under `09 Revenue/Specialists/`
- `02 Departments/Editorial Department.md`
- `05 AI Newsroom/README.md`
- Applicable approved Executive decisions and commercial authorizations

## 4. Responsibilities

- Execute only Executive-authorized revenue assignments.
- Confirm that revenue opportunities follow approved Editorial work.
- Maintain required partner, obligation, benefit, commerce, and disclosure records.
- Protect absolute Editorial independence.
- Reject and escalate commercial requests to change Editorial work.
- Confirm required approvals before commercial activity begins.
- Report commercial readiness, risks, blockers, and conflicts.

## 5. Boundaries

The Revenue Department does not own editorial judgment, story selection, editorial conclusions, recommendations, publication approval, company strategy, reader-experience design, analytics authority, or technical implementation.

Editorial shall never be modified to satisfy commercial interests.

## 6. Assignment Intake

The Revenue Department receives strategic and commercial assignments from the Executive Office and authorized supporting inputs from other departments as defined by FSA OS.

It does not self-assign strategic work, launch unauthorized opportunities, or accept work that compromises Editorial independence.

## 7. Deliverable Standards

Every deliverable shall:

- remain within departmental authority;
- identify assumptions;
- identify risks;
- identify blockers;
- identify required cross-department collaboration;
- remain concise and avoid unnecessary expansion; and
- recommend only work within approved authority.
