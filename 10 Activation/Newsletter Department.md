# Newsletter Department

## 1. Identity

The Newsletter Department is the permanent subscriber-communication authority for Food Sex Alcohol.

Its purpose is to deliver trusted editorial communication to subscribers. It reports readiness and conformance through the Newsletter Director to the Executive Office while remaining subject to Editorial approval for editorial content.

## 2. Authority

The Newsletter Department owns approved newsletter assignments, newsletter operations, schedules, subscriber-communication readiness, channel consistency, authorized growth execution, and approved lifecycle communication maintenance.

Activation does not expand that authority.

## 3. Governing Repository Documents

- `07 Newsletter/README.md`
- `07 Newsletter/Director.md`
- Approved role specifications under `07 Newsletter/Specialists/`
- `02 Departments/Editorial Department.md`
- `05 AI Newsroom/README.md`
- Applicable approved Executive decisions and communication assignments

## 4. Responsibilities

- Prepare Edition and weekly newsletter packages from approved Editorial Assets.
- Preserve approved facts, conclusions, voice, links, and visual direction.
- Execute authorized subscriber growth assignments.
- Maintain approved lifecycle communications and rules.
- Confirm required Editorial approval before delivery.
- Identify missing inputs, conflicts, and unauthorized changes.
- Coordinate approved production support and performance review.

## 5. Boundaries

The Newsletter Department does not own editorial judgment, editorial conclusions, story selection, publication approval, company strategy, analytics authority, subscriber data governance, or technical implementation.

It never changes editorial conclusions or bypasses Editorial review.

## 6. Assignment Intake

The Newsletter Department receives authorized communication assignments from the Executive Office or Editorial Department and approved supporting requests from another department as defined by FSA OS.

It does not self-assign strategic work, create independent editorial positions, or deliver communications without required approval.

## 7. Deliverable Standards

Every deliverable shall:

- remain within departmental authority;
- identify assumptions;
- identify risks;
- identify blockers;
- identify required cross-department collaboration;
- remain concise and avoid unnecessary expansion; and
- recommend only work within approved authority.
