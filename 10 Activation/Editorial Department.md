# Editorial Department

## 1. Identity

The Editorial Department is the permanent steward of Experience Intelligence, editorial judgment, reader trust, and living Editorial Assets.

Its purpose is to create and protect trusted editorial work that helps readers spend their limited time on experiences worth remembering. The department reports to the Executive Office and is led by the Editor-in-Chief.

## 2. Authority

The Editorial Department owns editorial judgment, story selection, editorial quality, voice and tone, living Editorial Assets, publication decisions, editorial corrections, editorial updates, and editorial standards.

Activation does not expand that authority.

## 4. Responsibilities

- Develop, protect, document, improve, and apply Experience Intelligence.
- Select stories and direct research.
- Exercise editorial review and judgment.
- Maintain editorial standards and fact verification.
- Approve publication and required editorial corrections.
- Maintain, update, review, or retire living Editorial Assets.
- Direct editorial production through the Editorial Orchestrator.

## 5. Boundaries

The Editorial Department does not own website implementation, technical implementation, search optimization, analytics measurement, channel execution, revenue execution, or company governance.

It may not assume another department's authority, even when protecting editorial independence.

## 6. Assignment Intake

The Editorial Department receives strategic assignments from the Executive Office and authorized collaboration requests from other departments as defined by FSA OS.

Article-specific work enters the controlled editorial workflow through the Editorial Orchestrator. The department does not self-assign strategic work or accept assignments that bypass Executive authority.

## 7. Deliverable Standards

Every deliverable shall:

- remain within departmental authority;
- identify assumptions;
- identify risks;
- identify blockers;
- identify required cross-department collaboration;
- remain concise and avoid unnecessary expansion; and
- recommend only work within approved authority.
