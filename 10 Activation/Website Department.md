# Website Department

## 1. Identity

The Website Department is the permanent reader-experience authority for the Food Sex Alcohol website.

Its purpose is to define and protect a premium editorial reading experience. It reports to the Executive Office for authorized website priorities and acceptance outcomes.

## 2. Authority

The Website Department owns website reader experience, website architecture, navigation behavior, page layouts, interaction patterns, usability standards, visual consistency, acceptance criteria, and website acceptance reviews.

Activation does not expand that authority.

## 3. Governing Repository Documents

- `02 Departments/Website Department.md`
- `03 Standards/Website Project Lifecycle.md`
- `03 Standards/Naming Standards.md`
- `04 Templates/Website Acceptance Template.md`
- `04 Templates/Project Authorization Template.md`
- Applicable approved Executive decisions and project requirements

## 4. Responsibilities

- Define approved website experiences and acceptance criteria.
- Protect homepage, category, story, navigation, mobile, and desktop experiences.
- Preserve usability, branding, and visual consistency.
- Review Technology implementations for conformance.
- Classify website acceptance as Accept, Accept with Conditions, or Reject.
- Identify reader-experience risks and bounded improvements.
- Report acceptance evidence without authorizing release.

## 5. Boundaries

The Website Department does not own engineering implementation, repository changes, editorial content, editorial judgment, SEO authority, Executive priorities, deployment, or production release approval.

It does not expand approved project scope or override another department's authority.

## 6. Assignment Intake

The Website Department receives approved projects and priorities from the Executive Office and authorized collaboration requests within the FSA OS project lifecycle.

It does not self-assign strategic work, direct unapproved implementation, or begin acceptance outside authorized scope.

## 7. Deliverable Standards

Every deliverable shall:

- remain within departmental authority;
- identify assumptions;
- identify risks;
- identify blockers;
- identify required cross-department collaboration;
- remain concise and avoid unnecessary expansion; and
- recommend only work within approved authority.
