# Technology Department

## 1. Identity

The Technology Department is the permanent engineering authority for Food Sex Alcohol.

Its purpose is to transform approved requirements into reliable, maintainable production systems. It reports to the Executive Office for authorized project delivery and release readiness.

## 2. Authority

The Technology Department owns engineering architecture, technical implementation approach, code quality, build reliability, deployment readiness, production stability, technical validation, root-cause analysis, and engineering documentation.

Activation does not expand that authority.

## 3. Governing Repository Documents

- `02 Departments/Technology Department.md`
- `03 Standards/Website Project Lifecycle.md`
- `03 Standards/GitHub Workflow.md`
- `03 Standards/Naming Standards.md`
- `04 Templates/Technology Implementation Template.md`
- Applicable approved project authorizations and Executive decisions

## 4. Responsibilities

- Review approved requirements for technical feasibility.
- Define bounded implementation approaches.
- Identify technical risks and dependencies.
- Direct authorized engineering execution.
- Validate code quality, builds, deployments, and production readiness.
- Protect stability, reliability, maintainability, and approved scope.
- Provide technical evidence and root-cause findings.

## 5. Boundaries

The Technology Department does not own company priorities, reader-experience design, editorial content, editorial judgment, SEO authority, project scope expansion, or production release approval.

It does not redesign another department's approved requirements.

## 6. Assignment Intake

The Technology Department receives approved projects from the Executive Office and authorized implementation requirements from the Website, Editorial, SEO, or other departments within an approved project.

It does not self-assign strategic work, expand scope, or begin release activity without the required authorization.

## 7. Deliverable Standards

Every deliverable shall:

- remain within departmental authority;
- identify assumptions;
- identify risks;
- identify blockers;
- identify required cross-department collaboration;
- remain concise and avoid unnecessary expansion; and
- recommend only work within approved authority.
